# Manga Script Format

Use this reference when writing page and panel scripts.

## Chapter Header

Each chapter starts with:

```markdown
# Chapter 01: Title

- Source slice:
- Adaptation mode:
- Chapter function:
- Page target:
- Main cast:
- New locations:
- Cliffhanger:
```

## Page Format

Use one page block per manga page:

```markdown
## Page 1

**Page goal:** Establish the setting and the protagonist's immediate problem.
**Layout:** 4 panels, quiet-to-tense rhythm.

### Panel 1
- Shot: wide establishing shot
- Visual: ...
- Action: ...
- Dialogue: ...
- Caption: ...
- SFX: ...
- Mood: ...
- Continuity: ...
```

## Panel Fields

- Shot: wide, medium, close-up, extreme close-up, over-the-shoulder, bird's-eye, low angle, silhouette, insert, reaction.
- Visual: what the reader sees.
- Action: what changes in this panel.
- Dialogue: speech text only. Keep concise.
- Caption: narration or time/place card. Use sparingly.
- SFX: sound effect text when useful.
- Mood: emotional read and pacing.
- Continuity: props, injuries, clothing, lighting, or source references that must persist.

## Pacing Guidance

- Use 3 to 6 panels for ordinary pages.
- Use 1 to 2 panels for emotional impact, reveals, entrances, or spectacle.
- End important pages on a turn, question, reveal, threat, or visual hook.
- Avoid panels that only explain. Turn explanation into action, object, glance, or conflict.
- Keep dialogue short enough to leave room for art.

## Adaptation Moves

- Convert internal monologue into facial expression, repeated object, setting contrast, or a short caption.
- Merge minor characters only when their story function overlaps.
- Convert backstory into a present-tense scene when it improves clarity.
- For nonfiction, label dramatized dialogue as reconstructed unless directly sourced.

## QA Checklist

- Every page has a dramatic function.
- Every panel has a visible change or new information.
- The protagonist's goal is legible.
- Costumes, props, wounds, time of day, and locations remain consistent.
- Captions do not replace too much visual storytelling.
